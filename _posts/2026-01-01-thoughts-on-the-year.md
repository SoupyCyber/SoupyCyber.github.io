Wow. Just wow. I don't know about ya'll but I am exhausted. I am flat out tired in that accumulatory "there-was-a-lot-packed-in" way, not the "burn-it-all-down" way...not yet xD

Professionally this year was dense. The threat actors did NOT slow down, Geopolitics did not stabilize and my personal and professional pursuit of actionable intelligence stayed pretty "top of mind" for the year. I honestly am having trouble *remembering* all the crazy stuff that happened this year, and I've been on holiday for at least a week. The call more deliverables and metrics and value statements come later in the year, but it drove a push in the "Ber" months that outpaced the standard cadence of the rest of the year. I will say, it drove a lot of collaboration with various teams in the last half!

In my personal life, there were more commitments, family events, kid activities and extracurriculars than any year yet. I don't know if you've ever heard the phrase before, but its true "The days are long, but the years are short." I felt that big-time this year.

So yeah, I'm tired. But, I'm still here! And thats really, really important.

I should clarify though; 2025 was not terrible...maybe a little borderline "bad" at some points, but mostly it was just FULL.

One of the bigger lessons I learned this year is...motivation is unreliable. Energy spikes, crashes and flat-out disappears without notice. What does work, and is *terribly* boring and not flashy whatsoever, is *Consistency.* Which has me thinking about 2026.

I've spent probably the last month thinking about what I want my plan to be for 2026. I went into 2025 thinking "this is gonna be my best year yet! I'm gonna make big changes and be a new me!" Welp, SPOILER ALERT! It didn't quite work out that time way. I mean don't get me wrong there but was a ton of growth, but I was focused on the wrong things.

So, In 2026 we hone in on consistency. I think for me that looks like those small wins every single day - repetition

Repetition without drama. Boring on purpose. Showing up even when I’m tired, ESPECIALLY when I’m tired. For me, I think consistency in 2026 looks like fewer grand declarations and more quiet follow-through. Writing regularly, even if it’s short. Reading threat reporting even when my brain wants to doomscroll. Keeping my tooling sharp. Keeping my notes clean. Drinking the coffee, doing the work, closing the loop. Not everything needs to be a “project.” Some things just need to be habits.

It also means being more realistic about capacity. I’m a CTI analyst, not a machine. I’m a dad, not a background NPC in my own life. There are seasons where output spikes and seasons where maintenance is the win. Pretending otherwise just leads to burnout with better branding. Though, I do plan on trying to build my own personal brand more.

Professionally, 2026 is about tightening fundamentals. Deepening tradecraft. Pursuing a few certifications I’ve had on the list for a while. I don't wanna collect acronyms, but I do want to sharpen how I think and how I explain threats to people who don’t live in this space every day. Better analysis. Clearer writing. Fewer “because vibes” assessments? More confidence in my conclusions because the process behind them is solid.

Personally, it’s the same theme. Be present. Be consistent. Pick the kids up and get to the thing on time. Show up. Accept that some nights the win is getting everyone fed and into bed and calling it a day. That counts.

I don’t need 2026 to be my “best year ever.” I just need it to be sustainable.

So yeah — I’m tired. Coffee helps. Dark humor helps. Experience helps. And consistency? That’s the plan. Not flashy. Not sexy. But effective.

Here’s to showing up again tomorrow. Farewell 2025, and hello 2026. Let's get better everyday yall.

Stay Soupy, Stay Cyber!
